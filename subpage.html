TAYLOR SWIFT - RED (TAYLOR’S VERSION)


Red (Taylor’s Version) is a collection of all the songs that have been written for the album Red, released originally in 2012, along with a few additional tracks. The Taylor’s Version, on the other hand, was released on November 12, 2021, 7 months after putting out her previous rerecording of another studio album of hers, Fearless.

The album consists of 30 songs, including all 19 tracks from the deluxe version of Red; six tracks “from the Vault” that were scrapped from the original release; an acoustic version of the intro State of Grace; the 10-minute, full version of Track 5 All Too Well; the charity single Ronan; and her iteration of the songs Babe and Better Man. She did not act as the lead artist in the original recordings of the last two songs.

DOJA CAT - PLANET HER


Planet Her is the third studio album by singer and rapper Amala Zlamini, better known by her stage name Doja Cat. Released on June 25, 2021, she had put the record out just after releasing her debut studio album Amala in 2018 and sophomore album Hot Pink in 2019, the latter of which having exploded in popularity as the decade was ending. Planet Her features collabs from artists like SZA, The Weeknd, Ariana Grande, JID, Young Thug, and Eve, and contains the singles Kiss Me More, You Right, and Need To Know.

Sonically, Planet Her is a versatile mix of various genres such as Afrobeats, rap, pop, and R&B. Lyrically, the album touches on several topics and themes, including femininity, sexuality, yearning, relationships, and romance.

SZA - CTRL


Ctrl is the widely-acclaimed debut studio album by R&B singer Solána Rowe, also known by her stage name SZA. Released on June 9, 2017, Ctrl features collabs from artists such as Kendrick Lamar, Travis Scott, Isaiah Rashad, and James Fauntleroy, and contains the singles Drew Barrymore, Love Galore, The Weekend, Broken Clocks, and Garden (Say It Like Dat).

The album explores a variety of themes, including but not limited to love, relationships, self-esteem, social media, and sexuality. An entrancing R&B and soul melody accompanies an effectively relatable, down-to-earth presentation manifest in its lyricism in order to explore the aforementioned issues.
